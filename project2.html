FESTIVALS

Andhra Pradesh
Sri Venkateswara Swami vari Brahmotsavam or Srivari Brahmotsavam is the most important annual fête celebrated at Sri Venkateswara Temple in Tirumala-Tirupati of Chittoor District in Andhra Pradesh, India.The event is conducted for nine days during Hindu Calendar month of Āśvina which falls in between the English months September and october.It is one of the famous Indian festivals.

Arunachal pradesh
“Losar” or The New Year festival, is the most important festival of Tawang District in Arunachal Pradesh. The Monpa Tribe celebrate this as the coming of a new year. The Monpas are the major portion of the population in the Tawang region of Arunachal Pradesh. The initial celebrations start with people cleaning their homes and discarding all the old items to welcome the festival.It is one of the famous Indian festivals.

Gujarat
In Gujarat an imaginary King is paraded through the village and the villagers take this opportunity to make fun of the King. Another unique variation of the celebration of Holi is found in this state. The unmarried girls create images of Goddess Gauri out of the ashes left by the bonfire of the night before. They create these images with the wishful thought of marriage.It is one of the famous Indian festivals.

Madhya Pradesh
Madhya Pradesh,During the Diwali Festival celebrations,decoration and lighting is done in Madhya Pradesh, with every market nicely decorated with lighting bulbs. Prior to the Diwali night, called the Dhan-teras all the shops remain open the whole night through. Balushais, khasta or crumbly doughnuts without holes are part of the traditional offerings at Diwali.It is said to be festival of lights.

Tamil Nadu
People from diverse regions, following varying beliefs confer equal importance to land and agriculture, the basic means of livelihood in India. One such festival commemorating farming and the harvest season is celebrated in Tamil Nadu with full enthusiasm. The festival has derived its name from a rice pudding called 'Pongal', made of jaggery, milk and freshly harvested rice.

Kerala
The first day of Onam celebration starts with people visiting temples in morning. It is believed that King Mahabali starts his preparations to descend from Pathala to Kerala on this day. The Onam celebrations across the state called Atthachamayam, starts off with a procession at Thrippunithura near Kochi, the place where Mahabali is believed is banished from earth.onam is the festival in kerala.
